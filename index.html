Today's Quiz

Who was the first Black woman to travel to space?

Correct!

Dr. Mae Jemison became the first Black woman to travel to space on September 12, 1992, aboard the Space Shuttle Endeavour. A physician and engineer, she spent more than a week in space conducting experiments. Before NASA, she served in the Peace Corps in West Africa. She continues to advocate for science education and has started multiple companies focused on technology and social issues.

History Archive

February 1, 1960

Greensboro Sit-Ins Begin

Four Black college students staged a sit-in at a segregated Woolworth's lunch counter in Greensboro, North Carolina, sparking a movement that spread to 55 cities across 13 states.

March 2, 1955

Claudette Colvin's Stand

15-year-old Claudette Colvin refused to give up her seat on a Montgomery bus, nine months before Rosa Parks. Her case became one of four used to challenge segregation in Browder v. Gayle.

August 28, 1963

March on Washington

Over 250,000 people gathered for the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom, where Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. delivered his iconic "I Have a Dream" speech.

June 21, 1964

Freedom Summer Murders

Civil rights workers James Chaney, Andrew Goodman, and Michael Schwerner were murdered by the KKK in Mississippi while registering Black voters during Freedom Summer.

May 17, 1954

Brown v. Board of Education

The Supreme Court unanimously ruled that racial segregation in public schools was unconstitutional, overturning Plessy v. Ferguson and its "separate but equal" doctrine.

December 1, 1955

Rosa Parks' Arrest

Rosa Parks was arrested for refusing to give up her seat to a white passenger on a Montgomery bus, catalyzing the 381-day Montgomery Bus Boycott.
